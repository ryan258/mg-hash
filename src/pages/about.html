Consulting

In 2011 Mike opened, Michael Gregory Consulting, LLC.

Mike has helped hundreds of clients resolve conflicts and negotiate winning solutions business to government (IRS), business to business and within businesses.

Mike is passionate about helping others resolve conflicts, negotiating winning solutions and inspiring leaders. Mike helps as a negotiator, facilitator, mediator (qualified mediator with the Minnesota Supreme Court) and by teaching others how to work with difficult people, de-escalate, listen effectively and build relationships. Mike inspires leaders and brings out the best in others.

Past History

IRS

Mike came to work for the IRS focusing on business valuation, valuing intangibles and analyzing research and development in companies after completing his MBA in finance.

Mike testified in US Tax Court twice.

Mike managed a litigation support unit of experts and expanded the unit from nine to 21 technical employees in three years with up to dozen cases docketed in US Tax Court, US Court of Federal Claims or US District Court at any one time.

Mike hired PhD economist and statistician team members and started up and led a research group analyzing all of the returns in the country to improve voluntary compliance nationally for four years.

Mike was tapped to be a controller and then to be part of a half dozen team members to work with the IRS CFO to refinance the IRS $10.5 billion budget and help reorganize the IRS into 13 new business units reducing the finance staff from 1,200 to 900 in the process.

Mike served as an executive and focused his last 11 years of his career as a territory manager for up to 23 states coordinating business valuation and other technical areas nationally.

US Army

Mike was an engineer and engineering study manager for four years with the US Army Corps of Engineers.

Mike’s breakthrough experience relating to conflict resolution took place when he came to a district as an acting assistant director with 1,200 employees that had over 300 unfair labor practice complaints, grievances and EEO complaints. Mike worked with the director to address these systemically partnering with leadership, Human Resources, Equal Employment Opportunity, Labor Relations and the union training strategic stakeholders in mediation techniques and processes over a 4 ½ month time frame and left with less than 30 remaining. The district had a coffee for Mike when Mike left. Coffees in similar situations previously took about an hour and maybe 20 people showed up. This coffee took over 6 hours with over 400 employees thanking Mike for making a real difference in their lives.
